Market Research

A successful approach that gains insignt into thought processes behind user behavior is audio-based user experience testing (typically accompanied by video screensharing). Showing the user a static (or existing) interface, they're asked to discuss aloud how they feel about solutions and why they arrived at that answer.

Case Studies

Investigate, research, and learn. Then use what you’ve learned; apply it in new ways.

Route End Feedback Loop

Improvements to where and when we ask users for feedback and reviews brought our store rating up, and consequently also brought up the app ranking and usage stats.

How and When to Ask

We had a simple display asking users to review after the 4th successful route completion. Route completions were below 20%, so seeing this at all was very rare. This meant that most users who rated the app were rating because they had a terrible experience. Not only that, but there was no clear way for users to provide feedback to us in a constructive way.

Learn & Pivot

We first added an option to provide feedback into an existing confirmation alert that is shown when a user exits navigation, allowing them to provide feedback, just exit nav, or cancel the action. The thinking here was to allow users to provide us feedback on a route that wasn’t working for them right at the moment that they are exiting navigation.

Due to the placement of this option, we saw a 10x spike in emails to our customer service team. Unfortunately, most were blank- indicating users were just clicking to get out of the navigation session rather than purposefully trying to provide feedback. We quickly removed this option to get users back on the right path there. This was obviously the wrong placement. Time to try again.

Final Product

A teammate brought up the idea of providing the same review/feedback option that we do when the user successfully arrives. We had since started showing this option for every successful arrival, it makes perfect sense to stay consistent here and provide the same unobtrusive options at the end of navigation, whether the user has successfully arrived or not. We ended with a consistent experience that offers users the option of rating or providing feedback after any route.

Measuring Success

Success was measured by the amount of positive reviews as well as the amount of actionable feedback received by our customer experience team. The ratings skyrocketed from 5 1-star reviews and just 3 5-star reviews (a 2.8 average for the version) to 10 1-star reviews and 64 5-star reviews (a 4.3 average for the version) in 2 releases.

As a bonus, the app’s ranking in the store increased because of the higher rating, causing downloads and UV’s to follow suit and shoot through the roof. Our >4-star rating KPI had been achieved.

Continued Iteration

We continued to iterate on this feedback loop, as well other areas of the app!
