Skills

I have a strongly t-shaped skillset. While focusing on development work, I can really get things done. Working with designers to perfect microinteraction and animations, building them out for proof of concept or just as a communication tool is a part of my job I enjoy the most.

My deep experience with accessibility principles has long driven my attitude towards semantic html. Using appropriate elements not only makes it easier for screenreader users but also for robots (SEO). Something about how it also affords the user more standardized interface elements- things like inputs, buttons, links, headings, and even video players are quickly recognizable and don't force the user to learn before being able to successfully interact with the app. I often liken html semantics to ramps on sidewalks, they have grown helpful beyond their original purpose and afford all users a better, easier to use world.

I place a high value on communication and feedback. Technical debt, diagrams, and documenting details in wikis and readmes is a big strength of my development process. I see code reviews as an integral part of any growing team. Reviews set a stage for teaching junior engineers how to discuss decisions, get feedback patterns, and ask questions. They can often be great discussion starters by fostering ideas for lunch and learn/brown bag sessions. I'll always respect and encourage engineers who want to be better engineers, and members of the development community.
